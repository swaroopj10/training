Breaks

We have already seen the p element, used to create a paragraph, but there are other types of breaks available in HTML.

You can jump ahead to Line Breaks, Pre-formatted Text, Horizontal Rule or Non-breaking space

Line Breaks

Explicit line breaks
can be used to break the line in a particular place.
Avoid these unless they are really needed.

They could be used to lay out poetry:

A Poem Never Says Anything

A poem never says anything.
It just opens a door, quietly.

Sleepless and bent
just like my aged father
waiting for me in a lonely winter night.

Uttaran Chaudhuri

Pre-formatted Text

That can also be done using the pre (pre-formatted) element, which is also often used to lay out code snippets. Unlike every other element, the pre element also respects whitespace.

The Lake Isle of Innisfree

        I will arise and go now, and go to Innisfree,
        And a small cabin build there, of clay and wattles made;
        Nine bean-rows will I have there, a hive for the honey-bee,
        And live alone in the bee-loud glade.

        And I shall have some peace there, for peace comes dropping slow,
        Dropping from the veils of the morning to where the cricket sings;
        There midnight’s all a glimmer, and noon a purple glow,
        And evening full of the linnet’s wings.

        I will arise and go now, for always night and day
        I hear lake water lapping with low sounds by the shore;
        While I stand on the roadway, or on the pavements grey,
        I hear it in the deep heart’s core.

W B Yeats

Horizontal Rule

Horizontal lines can be added as section breaks using the hr element. While these may seem decorative, they are generally interpreted as a break of content greater than a paragraph and less than a section (see later). Expect screen readers to treat them that way. There are other ways to introduce a decorative line.

The Road Not Taken

        Two roads diverged in a yellow wood,
        And sorry I could not travel both
        And be one traveler, long I stood
        And looked down one as far as I could
        To where it bent in the undergrowth;
            
        Then took the other, as just as fair,
        And having perhaps the better claim,
        Because it was grassy and wanted wear;
        Though as for that the passing there
        Had worn them really about the same,
            
        And both that morning equally lay
        In leaves no step had trodden black.
        Oh, I kept the first for another day!
        Yet knowing how way leads on to way,
        I doubted if I should ever come back.
            
        I shall be telling this with a sigh
        Somewhere ages and ages hence:
        Two roads diverged in a wood, and I-
        I took the one less traveled by,
        And that has made all the difference.

Robert Frost

Non-breaking Space

Since HTML does not respect whitespace (two or more consecutive whitespace characters are treated as a single space), we sometimes need to control how whitespace is represented. If we want to prevent a break from appearing in an inopportune place, we can use the non-breaking space.
